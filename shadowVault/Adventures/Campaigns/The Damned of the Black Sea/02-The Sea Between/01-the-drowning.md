# Act 1: The Drowning

## Act Overview

- **Purpose:** The prologue (~half a session). Deliver the seed of the whole adventure: the escape from the collapsing temple, Rauthorn's judgment on the beach, the party's death — staged so that death is certain but *how they die* is theirs — and the rebirth vision that hands them to the sea between.
- **State of the world:** Seconds after the party destroyed the [[shard-of-the-deep|Shard of the Deep]] (the destroyed-Shard ending of [[01-The Shard of the Deep/00-overview|Adventure I]]). The island convulses; the temple is coming down; the Leviathan is being born somewhere beneath the black water. On the beach, [[captain-rauthorn|Captain Rauthorn]] and the remaining crew are fighting off drowned things roused by the rebirth.
- **Ways it can end:** Only one way — the party dies on the beach and wakes aboard the [[the-halcyon|Halcyon]] ([[02-the-smooth-sea|Act 2]]). There is no path through this act alive, and the act is honest about everything except that.

> [!warning] Ambiguity begins here
> From the moment the party wakes on the ketch, nothing may confirm they died. Stage the beach so its ending is *felt* but never sealed with narration like "you are dead" — each PC's screen fades from a wound, cold water, and darkness. See the hard constraint in [[00-overview|the overview]].

## Scene: The Temple Comes Down

- **Purpose:** Seal every way out of the temple in one stroke and leave the vision pool as the only door.
- **Trigger:** The Shard shatters. The Chamber of the Shard begins to fall.
- **Setup:** The deepest rooms of [[03-the-temple-of-the-deep|Adventure I]]'s temple, shaking apart. The bluish veins in the walls flare and gutter like a failing pulse. The smell of deep water is rushing *up*.

### What Happens

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Shard's light does not go out — it goes *everywhere*. The chamber rings like a struck bell, and the veins in the black stone flare blinding-blue, then gutter like a lamp running dry. Somewhere far below, something vast turns over, and the whole island moves with it.
>
> The dome cracks. Salt water sprays from seams that were solid stone a heartbeat ago — and the smell hits you: deep water, cold and old, rushing *up* from below, as if the sea itself were climbing the temple to meet you.

The party flees back the way they came — and gets exactly one room. The Chamber of Splintered Truth is the first room on the way out, and it is the last the temple gives them: as they burst into it, the Corridor of the Wailing beyond collapses along its whole length. There is no way back through the temple. The pool is the only door.

**Hazard: The Corridor of the Wailing (falling)**
- Effect: the corridor's ghost-faces are screaming as the passage folds in on itself — the way out is gone before anyone can take three steps into it. A PC who insists on trying gets one round inside: DC 12 DEX check or 1d6 damage from falling rock, and the collapse drives them back into the chamber regardless.
- Counter: none. Beyond the corridor, the Gallery, the Steps, the Halls, and the Maw are already burying themselves — the whole island can hear it. Nothing leads out anymore.

> [!tip] GM Guidance
> Don't roll the collapse into a TPK — this scene kills nobody. Its job is momentum and the message *the temple is spending itself to push you one particular way*. When the corridor falls, let the smartest player say it first: the pool is the only door left.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Pool Opens
> Ahead of you, the Corridor of the Wailing folds in on itself like a throat swallowing, and its screaming stops. Dust rolls across the floor and settles. The round chamber is the only still place left in the world.
>
> The pool at its center is not showing visions anymore. It is *glowing* — a soft, deep, bluish light — and where your reflections should be, there is open water. Shallow water over pale sand, seen from beneath: a night sky rippling above the surface, and firelight — warm, orange, alive — dancing across it from somewhere on shore. Shapes move up there, blurred by the water: legs wading through surf, figures hurrying along a beach.


### Outcomes

- The party dives → surfaces in the shallows off the beach → The Beach.
- A PC refuses to dive → the chamber finishes collapsing around them; they wake in the surf with the others, coughing, with no memory of how (the sea does not let its debts drown *here* — never explain this).

## Scene: The Beach

- **Purpose:** The unwinnable fight. Rauthorn's judgment, the last-stand vignettes, and each PC's death — one mark of which they will carry for the rest of the adventure.
- **Trigger:** The party surfaces in the surf at the landing beach.
- **Setup:** Night, firelight, chaos. The Tideblade's boats are drawn up; the remaining crew fight in a ragged line against things wading out of the water. [[captain-rauthorn|Rauthorn]] holds the center, saber black in the firelight, blue light flickering in his eyes.

### What Happens

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You come up in the surf into a battle. The beach is torn firelight and running men — the Tideblade's crew, your crew, in a broken line along the waterline, and wading up out of the black water to meet them come sailors who did not sail with you: bloated, barnacled, streaming, with a green light behind their eyes like lanterns seen through fathoms.
>
> At the center of the line stands Captain Rauthorn, and where his saber passes, the drowned go down and *stay* down. He sees you in the surf. For one heartbeat something like relief crosses his face.
>
> Then the island shakes again, hard, and his eyes go north — to where the temple lies hidden behind the island's tangled green. You cannot see it from here. But now a glow rises from that hidden place and climbs into the night sky, a light that was not there before.

**Encounter: The Drowned Line**
- Enemies: 1d4+1 × [[drowned-sailor]] (per PC engaged, more wading out behind — this is a backdrop, not a set-piece)
- Tactics: the drowned grab and drag toward the water; they ignore the crew's dead and press for the living
- Terrain: surf is difficult ground; firelight in patches; overturned boats give cover
- Treasure: none

Let the party fight a real round or two beside their crewmates. Then Rauthorn reaches them, and asks — quiet, almost a whisper — what happened in the temple. **When the party tells him the Shard is destroyed** (or lies badly; he has heard the sea change), he goes very still.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Judgment
> "The sea knows no mercy," Rauthorn says, in the voice he uses for traitors. He is not shouting. That is the worst of it. "I gave you my ship, my chart, my one chance to never die. And you fed it to the water."
>
> The blue light in his eyes stops flickering and *holds*, steady as a lamp. Around you, the crew you fought beside are turning from the surf, blades still wet, faces closing like doors.
>
> "The sea knows no mercy," the captain says again, and raises his saber. "And neither do I."

**Hazard: The Captain's Judgment (unwinnable — no stat blocks by design)**
- Effect: Rauthorn attacks +7 (1d8+3 with the dark saber) and cannot be dropped — crew bodyguards absorb anything that would fell him, and the drowned keep coming at everyone's backs. Endless crew attack +4 (1d6), two or more per PC, always more. Real rounds, real rolls, real damage — the fight only flows one way.
- Counter: none. Fleeing inland meets drowned sailors; fleeing to sea meets the things they came from. The scene ends when the last PC falls.

**Last-stand vignettes.** When a PC drops to 0 HP, they do not simply die — they get a short spotlight: **one final act that matters and automatically succeeds** (shielding a friend so their last round is spent standing; hurling the captain's own words back at him; a defiant curse; a whispered prayer another PC hears). Play each in turn. **Record each PC's final act and last words** — Rauthorn will remember them in Adventure III. Then the wound lands — note *what* kills each PC (Rauthorn's saber, a drowned sailor's grip, cold water) — that mark is theirs for the whole adventure.

> [!tip] GM Guidance
> Death is certain; how they die is theirs — say this out loud at the table if the players start looking for the winning line that isn't there. Give them the dignity of the vignettes instead. Do not narrate past each PC's fade: wound, cold, dark. Nothing after.

### Outcomes

- The last PC falls → The Sea Between.

## Scene: The Sea Between

- **Purpose:** The rebirth vision — the only dream of the entire adventure — and the waking that starts Act 2.
- **Trigger:** All PCs are down.
- **Setup:** No place. Darkness, cold, the sensation of sinking.

### What Happens

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Rebirth Vision
> Darkness. Cold. The feeling of sinking, slow and endless, with no bottom to reach.
>
> Then the image you saw once in a pool of still water is around you and real: a storm-torn black sea, and out of it a figure rising — vast beyond ships, beyond cliffs, water falling from its flanks like collapsing coastlines. Its eyes burn like two cold stars. It is not waking. It is being *born* — and the sea itself recoils from it.
>
> Its gaze finds you. Each of you. Something passes through you that is not words, but it lands the way words do, and what it feels like — as close as you will ever be able to say — is a debt being acknowledged.
>
> Then something vast slides past you in the dark, close enough to touch: an eye longer than a boat, watching you all the way past.
>
> Cold. Dark. And then — wood, under your cheek. Warm, dry wood, gently rising and falling. Somewhere above you, a rope creaks. Then again — a different creak. Then a third. In order. Like a heartbeat.

> [!tip] GM Guidance
> This is the **only dream in the whole adventure** — aboard the ketch they will sleep and never dream, and that contrast is itself a clue. It also stays deniable: a drowning dream, the vision pool's last echo. Never rule on it. When the third creak sounds, go to [[02-the-smooth-sea|Act 2]].

### Outcomes

- The party wakes aboard the [[the-halcyon|Halcyon]] → [[02-the-smooth-sea|Act 2: The Smooth Sea]].
