# The Sleeping Man

- **Role:** passenger — secretly the largest of the Leviathan's embodied gifts; the biggest gift sleeps deepest
- **Appearance:** A big man asleep in the driest corner of the passenger hold, curled around a **sea chest nobody has ever seen opened**. (His signature object; never call it out mechanically.) His face is peaceful the way harbors are peaceful.
- **Personality:** None on display — **he never wakes.** Not for boarding alarms, not for the storm, not for shaking, shouting, or cold water. *Mannerism:* he **breathes in time with the ship's three creaks** — a detail for the third or fourth session, when a player finally sits with him long enough to notice.
- **Motivation:** Nothing — he never wakes. The other passengers step around him fondly, as around a fixture. Even they don't remember him boarding — *he* is the only passenger nobody remembers **at all**.
- **Secrets:** (GM only) He embodies the gift of **a rare sea-flavored magic item of real weight for every PC**, wrapped in oilcloth in the chest he guards with his sleep. Nobody learns what he is until the end — if he crosses, his chest turns up under [[the-gift-boat|the gift boat's]] aft bench in Adventure III. The chest cannot be opened aboard the ketch: no lock is visible, no seam gives, and prying at it wakes nothing and achieves nothing (the old man, watching: *"Let him keep it shut. It's his to carry."*). If given to the Undertow, Mooring-Men bear him to the rail still sleeping, chest and all — the gentlest and most terrible of the sacrifices, gone without ever having said a word — and the whole party's item gift is deleted from the game.
- **Relationships:** Gift of [[the-old-sailor|the ferryman]]; fellow passenger to [[the-fencing-master]], [[the-quiet-girl]], [[the-gambler]], [[the-cartographer]].
- **Stat block:** none — he sleeps.
