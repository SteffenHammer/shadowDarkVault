# The Gambler

- **Role:** passenger — secretly one of the Leviathan's embodied gifts; sacrifice currency at the Toll
- **Appearance:** A man in a good coat gone shabby, quick-eyed, unshaven with precision. His hands are never still and never hurried.
- **Personality:** Wry, companionable, unbeatable — and strangely unbothered by being unbeatable. *Mannerism:* he **rolls strange, blank-faced coins across his knuckles**, hand after hand, coins from no mint anyone can name. (His signature object; never call it out mechanically.) He **wins every hand and always folds the last one** — asked why, he says: *"Never take the last thing a man has. Bad table manners. Worse luck."*
- **Motivation:** To keep his loop: cards every dog-watch, anyone's rules, the party's **thief** his favorite mark and favorite student — he'll teach them his fold-tell and swear them to secrecy. He doesn't know what he is.
- **Secrets:** (GM only) He embodies **"Luck of the Deep" — an additional luck token for every PC** (the gambler's coin; potentially recharging at sea in later adventures). His wrong memory of boarding: *"a debt paid at the dockside table"* — which dock, which debt, and who paid whom shift with each telling. Asked his name, he deals instead. If given to the Undertow he shrugs, pockets his coins, tips an imaginary hat and walks to the rail — the coins vanish with him: the whole party's luck gift is deleted, discovered only as an absence aboard [[the-gift-boat]] in Adventure III.
- **Relationships:** Gift of [[the-old-sailor|the ferryman]]; fellow passenger to [[the-fencing-master]], [[the-quiet-girl]], [[the-cartographer]], [[the-sleeping-man]]. Nobody remembers him embarking — he claims *they* were all late.
- **Stat block:** none needed — a steady hand at storm-stations in Act 4 (auto-succeeds DC 12 station checks; he says the odds owe him).
