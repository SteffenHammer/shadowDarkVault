# The Pilot

- **Role:** liar — the face of the Undertow; boards at Night 3 with the "faster way home"
- **Appearance:** A small, neat, *dry* man — the only dry thing in the fog — in a good coat with brass buttons, carrying a ledger. His smile is exactly right and stays exactly right a half-second too long. His trim pilot boat is crewed by six hooded oarsmen who row in perfect silence, and she leaves **no wake**.
- **Personality:** Reasonable, unhurried, professionally warm. He never begs, never threatens, answers every doubt with paperwork. *Mannerism:* he consults a pocket-watch that has no hands, nods as if satisfied, and puts it away.
- **Motivation:** To get souls to step, willingly, off the ferryman's deck and into his boat. A willing step is the cheapest collection there is. He works the doubters first — anyone who has voiced distrust of [[the-old-sailor|the old man]] hears it quoted back, gently, in their own words. The fog was listening.
- **Secrets:** He is [[the-undertow|the Undertow]] reaching forward — his "channel" and "license" are set dressing over a mouth. His ledger holds every PC's name written fair ("manifest," he says), and a page-corner turned down at five more names nobody can quite read. He cannot enter the lantern's light and will not name the ferryman: *"Your… helmsman. We're aware of him."* Anyone who boards his boat is collected — final.
- **Relationships:** Finger of [[the-undertow]]; his oarsmen are [[pilots-boatman|his boatmen]]. Professional disdain for the loud methods of the [[mooring-man|Mooring-Men]].
- **Stat block:** [[the-pilot-unmasked]] — only once the mask comes off ([[03-the-fog-banks|Act 3: The Pilot's Offer]]).
