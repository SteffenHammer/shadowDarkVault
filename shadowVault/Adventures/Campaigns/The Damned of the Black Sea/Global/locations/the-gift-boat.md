# The Gift Boat

A small, weathered fishing boat riding at anchor in the shallows of the beach where the party fell — seaworthy and empty, no crew, no name on her stern, provisions and water stowed for exactly the party's number. Her ropes are dry when everything else on the beach is soaked.

- **Type:** ship — the party's vessel from the end of Adventure II onward
- **Notable features:**
	- She has the [[the-halcyon|Halcyon's]] own lines and the same patched tan sail — as if the ship that carried them had a little sister, or had shrunk down to something the living world could hold.
	- Sail her long enough and she creaks — the same three creaks, in the same order, like a heartbeat the party already knows.
	- Low on her transom, half-hidden under old paint: a tiny scratched wave-mark — the *"born in the depths of the sea"* symbol from the Gate of Three Bindings.
	- Everything about her is deniable: a boat lost from the Tideblade in the storm; a fisherman's craft driven here and abandoned. Nothing about her insists otherwise. Ever.
- **Inhabitants & factions:** none — she waits.
- **Secrets (GM only):**
	- **She is the ferryman's last gift, and her stores are the treasure of Adventure II** — stowed aboard in mundane, deniable form, one cargo per passenger who *survived* the crossing: the Fencing Master's exotic unlabeled bottle (one deep pull each — a permanent attribute point/talent); the Sleeping Man's sea chest under the aft bench, items wrapped in oilcloth (a rare sea-flavored magic item each); the Quiet Girl's bundle of knotted cords (a new ability each); the Gambler's small purse of strange coins, one per PC ("Luck of the Deep" — a re-roll token, potentially recharging at sea); the Cartographer's map rolled in a bone tube lashed under the gunwale (coastlines that don't exist yet, marking where Rauthorn's ritual will happen).
	- **Discovering and redeeming the cargo is Adventure III's opening — do not resolve it in Adventure II.** A sacrificed passenger's cargo simply is not aboard; nothing marks the empty space. Its absence is how the party learns, days later, exactly what they spent. The count is always exactly right for the party.
	- Whether she is an ordinary boat that happens to resemble the ketch, or something the ferryman left behind, is **never settled**. If she is ever wrecked or given up, the sea takes her quietly — no wreckage is ever found.
- **Appears in:** [[04-the-storm-crossing|Adventure II: The Landing]]; the party's vessel throughout Adventure III (they'll need her to follow the Cartographer's map).
