# The Tideblade

Once the flagship of a feared pirate fleet — built of dark tropical wood with a reinforced keel and a slim, fast hull, notorious for her speed, her agility in storms, and her brutal striking power. Legend among pirates says she was a colonial warship that Rauthorn took in a bloody mutiny; she has never entered a harbor since. Among traders they say: *"If you see her sails, it is already too late."*

## The Ship

- Three-master with square rig, plus lateen sails on the mizzen
- Broad main deck with raised quarterdeck and sheltered helm
- Captain's cabin with chart room, officers' quarters, crew deck with hammocks, cargo hold, powder magazine, brig

| Feature | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Length | ~38 m |
| Beam | ~9 m |
| Draft | ~3.5 m |
| Decks | 3 (upper, lower, hold) |
| Masts | 3 |
| Sail area | ~800 m² |

Larger than a typical brigantine, smaller than a ship of the line — optimized for speed and striking power, not long-range comfort.

## Armament

| Weapon | Count |
| --- | --- |
| Broadside cannons | 12 (6 per side) |
| Bow chasers | 2 |
| Swivel guns | 4 (deck-mounted) |
| Powder stores | ~20 barrels |

The cannons are modified for fast reloading; the bow chasers can fire chain shot or incendiaries; the swivel guns fire grapeshot against boarders.

## Marked by the Ritual (later in the campaign)

As the ritual progresses, the Tideblade changes — this is her state in later adventures, not in Adventure I:

A three-masted warship of dark, salt-crusted wood that glides over the sea at night like a shadow. Her sails are tattered but veined with a ghostly glow, as if the Black Sea itself drove her. The bow bears a Leviathan's jaw of real bone. The planks do not creak — they *whisper*. The deck lanterns burn with cold, blue light that gives no warmth, and runes are scratched into the keel in a language even experienced mages cannot read.

- **Ritual anchor:** bound to the ritual — she can only fully appear in a storm.
- **Soul chamber:** a sealed cabin where Rauthorn communes with the Black Sea.
- **Ghost crew:** some of the sailors are no longer alive; they obey while the ritual is unfinished.
- **Shroud:** in fog or at night, nearly invisible to normal senses.
- **Curse:** whoever touches the helm without Rauthorn's leave hears the voices of the drowned.
