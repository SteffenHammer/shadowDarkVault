# Interview Themes

Rounds run in this order; each theme fills its matching section of `concept-template.md`. The Logline has no theme — it is written last, at the ready gate. Probes are starting points: follow the answers wherever they lead, but a round only closes when its vagueness test passes.

## 1. Premise & Truth

Goal: the player-facing hook, the GM-facing truth, and the gap between them.

Probes:
- What do the players believe they are signing up for — and who asks them?
- What is actually going on underneath?
- Why has nobody solved this already?
- What is the first concrete thing the party notices that hints the premise isn't the whole story?

Vagueness test: premise and truth differ in at least one load-bearing fact, and the hook names who pulls the party in and why them.

## 2. Stakes & Clock

Goal: what failure or stalling costs the world.

Probes:
- If the party does nothing for a week, what has gotten worse?
- What can be lost permanently — and when?
- Who feels the stakes besides the party?

Vagueness test: the clock has at least one dated, visible escalation ("on the third night…"), not just a mood of urgency.

## 3. Antagonist & Threat

Goal: the opposing will, its motive, and its logic.

Probes:
- Who or what opposes the party — a person, a faction, a force?
- It wants ___ because ___ — fill both blanks.
- What does it do the first time the party interferes?
- Can it be bargained with, and at what price?

Vagueness test: the motive would still make sense if the party never showed up.

## 4. Cast

Goal: everyone who matters that the party will talk to — name, want, role.

Probes:
- Who can help, and what do they want in exchange?
- Who lies to the party, and why?
- Who changes sides under pressure?

Vagueness test: every named NPC has a want the party can satisfy or frustrate — nobody's role is "flavour".

## 5. Locations

Goal: where the story happens, each place with one signature detail.

Probes:
- Where does the party start, and where does the finale happen?
- Which location is the poster image of this adventure?
- One sensory detail per place — what does it smell, sound, or feel like?

Vagueness test: no location could be swapped for a generic one without losing a scene.

## 6. Structure & Beats

Goal: the spine, in play order, sized to the adventure type.

Probes:
- One-shot: what are the 4–6 scenes, and which is the set piece?
- Single adventure: what are the acts, and how does each end?
- Campaign: what are the adventures, what arcs across all of them, what threads carry between them?
- Where is the midpoint reversal — the moment the premise cracks and the truth shows?
- What choice does the finale force?

Vagueness test: every beat names what the party *does* there, and the finale is a decision, not just a fight.

## 7. Opposition Palette

Goal: the roster of fights and hazards, roughly levelled.

Probes:
- What does the party fight, and how dangerous is it relative to their level?
- Which threats are hazards — dangers you survive rather than kill?
- What is the signature creature nobody has seen before?

Vagueness test: the palette spans the whole adventure (a combat-free act is fine only if deliberate), and rough LVs sit inside the party's level range — top of range +2 for a boss.

## 8. Rewards

Goal: what the party gains — gold, items, leverage, knowledge.

Probes:
- What does the finale pay out?
- Is there an item that matters beyond its mechanics?
- What information is itself a reward?

Vagueness test: at least one reward is specific to this adventure, not generic loot.

## 9. Tone & Table

Goal: the constraints the build must respect.

Probes:
- Three words for the tone at the table.
- What content is off-limits?
- Level range, party size, session count — final answers.

Vagueness test: levels, party size, and sessions carry concrete values (a range like 1–3 counts; "whatever fits" doesn't).

## 10. Open Threads

Goal: hooks deliberately pointing beyond this adventure.

Probes:
- What question stays unanswered on purpose?
- Which NPC or faction could return?

Vagueness test: "none" is acceptable — but only stated explicitly.
